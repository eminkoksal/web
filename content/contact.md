## Content & Structure Notes

- **No contact form by default.** Email is enough. Forms add spam, friction, and a sense of corporate distance that the brand doesn't want. If a form is added later, it should be a single-field "what are you working on?" with a button — nothing more.
- **No phone number.** Discoverable phone numbers attract junk; the contact email handles real inquiries reliably.
- **Sticky `mailto:` button** (optional): a small floating button that opens the default mail client. Useful on mobile.
- **Anti-spam:** consider obfuscating the email in HTML (or rendering it via JS) to reduce scraper exposure — the visible text can stay as written.
