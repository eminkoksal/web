## Tone & Voice

### Pillars

- **Professional, data-driven** — every claim earns a number, a source, or a study citation.
- **Semi-academic** — comfortable with technical terms when they're load-bearing; explains them when they're not. Cites work directly. Doesn't talk down.
- **Enthusiastic** — present-tense, active verbs, clear convictions. The brand believes in the work and shows it. No hedging, no corporate-speak.

### Tonal targets

- Sound like a professor who consults — knows the literature, knows the boardroom, hasn't lost the spark.
- Closer to *Harvard Business Review* + *The Economist* than to a tech startup or a Big Four deck.
- **Lead with academic credibility, then show AI fluency** — in that order. Most consultants oversell AI and undersell rigor; most academics undersell urgency. The brand does both, but in sequence: the academic record earns the right to speak, and the AI work shows the speaking is current.

### English (primary)

- Active voice. Present tense.
- Concrete over abstract. "Analyzed 3,369 decisions" beats "performed extensive analysis."
- Short sentences pull. Long sentences explain. Mix them.
- Show conviction. "I think this is the right approach" → "This is the right approach, and here's why."
- Numbers in numerals (5, not "five") for figures. Stats are the protein of this voice.

### Turkish (secondary)

- Used for some long-form content and select operational guides.
- Same pillars: precise, evidence-led, warm. Avoid academic Ottomanisms.

### Example Phrasing

| Instead of… | Use… |
|-------------|------|
| "I am an expert in…" | "I specialize in…" |
| "I have many years of experience" | "Twenty years in competition economics — and now AI." |
| "I do AI consulting" | "I help organizations rebuild analytical workflows around AI." |
| "I published papers" | "I've published 50+ papers, including a Routledge volume on Türkiye's digitalisation." |
| "AI can really help you" | "AI cuts a quarter-century of decision review from months to days. We tested it." |

## Rationale

**Why one audience.** Focus is the simplest premium signal. Speaking to organizations only — not also to individual coaching clients — sharpens every page. The brand becomes legible: *this is who I work with, this is what I do, this is what it costs you not to.* Within that focus, the work runs on two reinforcing tracks (AI strategy for organizations + AI-native research), which together explain why the same person does both.

**Why headline both identities equally.** "Academic Economist & AI Consultant" is intentionally balanced rather than ordered. An academic identity alone reads as historic; an AI identity alone reads as opportunistic. The pairing — and the order of *academic credibility first, then AI fluency* in every supporting text — signals a researcher whose work is contemporary, not a consultant who once published.

**Why semi-academic, enthusiastic voice.** The "Academic Economist" half of the identity asks for rigor and citation. The "AI Consultant" half asks for momentum and conviction. The mix — careful but spirited — is the actual differentiator: most consultants undersell rigor; most academics undersell urgency. This brand does both, and in a deliberate order: academic credibility first, AI fluency layered on top.
